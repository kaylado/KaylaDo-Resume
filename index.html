Enter I am first year psychology student at the University of California, Riverside. I am also planning to minor in education to pursue a career of a school psychologist. I want to be able for people to succeed emotionally, academically, and socially. I want to offer support and care to my clients which allow me to assist those who struggle with mental health, relationship, and domestic problems. I want to study and treat people with emotional difficulties or mental illness. I am interested in minoring in education since I heard that is a very good idea to minor in to become a school psychologist due to it being in a school related environment. Doing that will help me connect with the teachers since I know how the classroom situation works. I know I would need to constantly consult with the teachers, the students' parents, and other psychologists outside of school to help many of my students.

I am a member of the Psi Chi Psychology Honors Club at the University of California, Riverside. It is a great way to get my foot in the door. It is very convenient to get a head start and attend many meetings that would consist of other members who have similar goals as me. It gives me many opportunities to listen to other psychologist guest speakers to hear about their experiences and to make connections since that is essential to life. I enjoy meeting new people who also major in psychology who are passionate as I am about the subject.

I am part of the The School Service Provision Research Collaborative (SSPRC) lab which is dedicated to conducting high-quality, applied research to effect enduring improvements to universal and targeted academic, behavioral, and social-emotional supports in schools as well as the processes and procedures used in their implementation. The work of the SSPRC is grounded in a prevention, early intervention, and multi-tiered problem-solving approach to service delivery. This work is driven by the understanding that improving factors influencing school service provision can impact the short- and long-term outcomes for students in school and beyond.
